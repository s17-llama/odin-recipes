Karak Chai

Description

Roughly translated, karak chai means 'strong tea'. It differs from Masala chai by using fewer spices, slightly stronger tea, evaporated milk, and lump sugar. I make up a big batch in the morning and drink it throughout the day. It's nice chilled or reheated.

Chai Away!

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Assam tea bags
  • Ceylon tea bags
  • Chinese lump white sugar
  • Ginger
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Rose buds
  • Cardamom
  • Milk

Steps

  1. Heat water in a small saucepan until bubbles start to form around the edges. Add tea bags. Reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Lightly crush lump sugar, ginger, cinnamon, rose buds, and cardamom pods in a mortar. Stir into the pot.
  3. Simmer until the tea bags and spices have become saturated, about 3 minutes. Add evaporated milk. Stir and simmer, without boiling, until sugar has dissolved and tea reaches the desired strength, about 8 minutes for strong tea.
  4. Strain into mugs.
